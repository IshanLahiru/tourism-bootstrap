SIGIRIYA ROCK FORTRESS

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century. Famous for its frescoes, water gardens, and lion-pawed entrance, it stands as one of Sri Lanka’s most iconic landmarks.

MIRISSA WHALE WATCHING

Set off into the Indian Ocean from the golden beaches of Mirissa to witness majestic blue whales, playful dolphins, and other marine life. Best experienced between November and April, it's a true oceanic adventure.

YALA NATIONAL PARK SAFARI

Home to the highest density of leopards in the world, Yala National Park offers thrilling safari rides through jungles and grasslands where you can spot elephants, crocodiles, peacocks, and many more wild species.

NUWARA ELIYA – HILL COUNTRY CHARM

Known as ‘Little England,’ Nuwara Eliya is surrounded by lush tea plantations, misty mountains, and colonial-era architecture. Enjoy scenic train rides, strawberry farms, and the beauty of Horton Plains National Park.

Kandy Esala Perahera: A Cultural Spectacle In Motion

Kandy Esala Perahera – Sri Lanka's Grandest Cultural Festival. The Kandy Esala Perahera, also known as the Festival of the Tooth, is one of the most spectacular cultural events in the world. Held annually in the sacred city of Kandy, this centuries-old tradition is a vibrant display of Sri Lanka's heritage, spirituality, and artistry. Every year, thousands of devotees and tourists from across the globe gather to witness the breathtaking spectacle of drummers, dancers, fire-breathers, and magnificently adorned elephants parading through the city streets.

Historical Significance

The festival dates back to the 3rd century BC when Buddhist traditions and the worship of the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha were deeply intertwined with Sri Lanka's monarchy. According to history, the relic was brought to Sri Lanka from India in the 4th century AD and became a symbol of royal authority. The Perahera is held in honor of the Sacred Tooth Relic, which is enshrined in thei Sri Dalada Maligawa (Temple of the Tooth) in Kandy. The Esala Perahera also coincides with the Esala full moon, a time believed to commemorate the Buddha's first sermon. It blends Buddhist rituals with the ancient Kohomba Kankariya, a traditional Kandyan dance ceremony intended to invoke blessings for rain, prosperity, and protection trom mistortune.

When and Where:

Location: Kandy, Central Province, Sri Lanka

Time: Usually held in July or August, spanning 10 days, culminating on the Esala full moon Poya day

Duration: Consists of 5 Kumbal Peraheras (processions) followed by 5 Randoli Peraheras, with increasing grandeur each night
